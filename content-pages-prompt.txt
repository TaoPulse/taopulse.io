## Pages to build

### Page 1: /emissions — "How TAO Emissions Work"
File: app/emissions/page.tsx

Content to cover (beginner-friendly, no jargon dumps):
- What emissions are: 0.5 TAO per block, ~3,600 TAO/day total
- Two stages: Injection (per block into subnet pools) → Distribution (per tempo, ~72 min, via Yuma Consensus)
- The Flow-Based Model ("Taoflow") — active since Nov 2025. Emissions now based on net TAO staking inflows (EMA over ~87 days), not price. Subnets with net outflows = zero emissions.
- Within-subnet split: 18% subnet creator, 41% validators, 41% miners
- What this means for stakers: you earn a slice of the validator's 41%, minus the validator's "take" (default 18% of delegated emissions)
- Simple visual: animated emission flow diagram (use divs/CSS, no chart library)
- Link to /halving and /staking

### Page 2: /dtao — "What is Dynamic TAO (dTAO)?"
File: app/dtao/page.tsx

Content to cover:
- dTAO launched late 2024 — the big shift from one TAO to subnet-specific alpha tokens
- Classic staking (root): stake TAO to validator → stay in TAO, earn TAO yield, no alpha exposure, lower risk
- dTAO subnet staking: TAO goes into subnet liquidity pool → you receive alpha tokens → alpha has its own price → earn more if subnet grows, but face impermanent loss risk
- Alpha halving: each subnet has its own halving schedule (independent of TAO halving)
- Who should use dTAO vs classic: clear recommendation table
- Key risks: impermanent loss, subnet failure, alpha token volatility
- Link to /staking and /subnets
